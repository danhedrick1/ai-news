## 🔬 Research Radar

- **Me, Myself, and π: Evaluating and Explaining LLM Introspection** — Proposes a taxonomy distinguishing genuine meta-cognition from surface-level self-simulation in LLMs; directly relevant to alignment and interpretability. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20276)

- **Enhancing Safety of LLMs via Embedding Space Separation** — Tackles the linear separability of harmful vs. safe embeddings that attackers exploit, proposing a defense that widens the gap. Timely given ongoing jailbreak research. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20206)

- **Locally Coherent Parallel Decoding in Diffusion Language Models** — Addresses the core weakness of diffusion LMs (independent token sampling) with a method that captures joint distributions, improving code generation quality. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20216)

- **Fast-Slow Thinking Reward Model** — Bridges cheap scalar reward models with expensive generative ones via a fast-slow routing system, cutting RLHF compute while maintaining accuracy. Practical for anyone doing alignment at scale. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20212)

- **AgentComm-Bench: Stress-Testing Cooperative Embodied AI Under Real Network Conditions** — Finally, a benchmark that tests multi-agent systems under latency, packet loss, and bandwidth collapse rather than idealized comms. Essential for robotics deployment. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20285)

- **Thinking into the Future: Latent Lookahead Training for Transformers** — Allows models to explore multiple plausible continuations before committing to a token, addressing a fundamental limitation of next-token prediction. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20219)

## 📊 Trend Watch

**The Sora shutdown is the loudest signal yet that video generation isn't paying its way.** OpenAI walking away from both a flagship product and a billion-dollar Disney partnership says more about the economics of generative video than any benchmark ever could. The inference costs for video models remain brutal, user retention has been mediocre, and the copyright landscape is a minefield. Don't be surprised if other labs quietly deprioritize their video offerings in the coming months.

**Agentic AI is the new battleground — and it's moving fast.** In a single news cycle: Anthropic ships autonomous computer control in Claude Code/Cowork, OpenAI launches agentic shopping in ChatGPT, and Arm designs its first-ever chip specifically for inference workloads powering agents. The stack is solidifying: inference silicon at the bottom, orchestration frameworks in the middle, consumer-facing agents at the top. The question is no longer "will agents happen" but "who controls the transaction layer." OpenAI and Google fighting over who gets to buy you jeans through a chatbot is both absurd and telling.

**Jensen's AGI claim deserves an eye-roll and a closer look.** The eye-roll: "AGI" has become a marketing term untethered from any agreed-upon benchmark. The closer look: Nvidia's CEO making this claim aligns the company's narrative with customers who need to justify massive GPU spend — if we've achieved AGI, then the capex was worth it. Watch for this framing to become standard among infrastructure providers. Meanwhile, the Stanford delusions research and the Interconnects piece on lossy self-improvement offer a useful corrective: the systems are powerful and getting more capable, but the failure modes are real and the improvement curves are sublinear. That's the nuanced story worth tracking.
