## Trend Watch

**The autonomy dial is turning up — carefully.** Anthropic's new auto mode for Claude Code, MIT's eBook on AI agent autonomy, and the broader industry conversation around agentic AI all point to the same inflection point: we're moving from AI as a suggestion engine to AI as an executor. But every major player is telegraphing caution — guardrails, leashes, safety checks. The question is no longer *whether* AI agents will act independently, but how much rope they'll be given and who bears liability when things go wrong.

**Meanwhile, the physical footprint of AI is meeting real-world resistance.** A Kentucky farmer turning down $26 million is a vivid symbol of a growing bottleneck: the AI industry's insatiable hunger for land, power, and infrastructure is colliding with communities that don't want to be turned into server farms. At the same time, Arm's move to produce its own chips with Meta suggests the hardware supply chain is restructuring around AI-first priorities. As software capabilities race ahead, the constraints are increasingly physical — energy, silicon, geography, and the humans who live on the land where the data centers would go.
